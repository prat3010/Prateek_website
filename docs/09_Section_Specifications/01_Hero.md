# **Hero Section**

## **Purpose**

The Hero Section is the first meaningful interaction between the visitor and Adaptive Portfolio.

It should immediately communicate confidence, clarity, and craftsmanship without overwhelming the visitor.

The Hero should not attempt to explain everything.

Its responsibility is to create curiosity, establish trust, and encourage exploration.

If every other section disappeared, the Hero alone should still communicate who I am, what I do, and why the visitor should continue.

---

# **Product Philosophy**

The Hero is not an advertisement.

It is a conversation starter.

Rather than making exaggerated claims, it should create enough confidence that visitors naturally want to learn more.

The Hero should feel calm.

Confident.

Intentional.

Premium.

---

# **Visitor Goals**

After viewing the Hero, every visitor should understand:

- who I am
- what I build
- why this website feels different
- what to do next

The Hero should answer these questions within the first few seconds.

---

# **First Visit Behaviour**

Before the Hero is displayed, the visitor is asked:

**Who are you here as?**

The selected Communication Identity becomes active.

Only after this selection should the Hero appear.

This immediately demonstrates the Adaptive Portfolio philosophy.

---

# **Hero Objectives**

The Hero should accomplish the following:

Establish identity.

Create curiosity.

Build trust.

Communicate professionalism.

Introduce adaptation.

Encourage exploration.

Everything else is secondary.

---

# **Information Hierarchy**

Information should appear in approximately this order.

1. Identity

Who I am.

---

2. Purpose

What I build.

---

3. Value

Why it matters.

---

4. Primary Call To Action

What the visitor should do next.

---

5. Secondary Call To Action

Optional exploration.

---

# **Communication Identity**

## **Hiring a Developer**

The Hero should communicate:

- engineering mindset
- curiosity
- product thinking
- craftsmanship
- continuous learning

The visitor should immediately understand:

“I build software thoughtfully.”

Avoid unnecessary business language.

---

## **Need a Website**

The Hero should communicate:

- trust
- professionalism
- collaboration
- premium execution
- business value

The visitor should immediately understand:

“This is someone I can confidently hire.”

Avoid unnecessary technical terminology.

---

## **Azure**

- energetic
- optimistic
- exploratory
- modern
- creative

The copy should inspire curiosity.

---

## **Noir**

The Hero should feel:

- premium
- elegant

The copy should inspire confidence.

---

# **Adaptive Behaviour**

The Hero should adapt:

- headline
- supporting copy
- primary CTA
- secondary CTA

The layout should remain consistent.

The visual identity should remain consistent.

Only the communication should change.

---

# **Hero Content**

The Hero should contain:

- headline
- supporting paragraph
- primary CTA
- secondary CTA
- profile image or visual identity
- optional status indicator

Every content element should be editable through the Content Management System.

---

# **Calls To Action**

Developer Mode examples:

- View Projects
- Download Resume
- Explore Experience

Business Mode examples:

- Start a Project
- View Services
- Download Quotation
- Get a Quote

The exact wording should remain editable.

---

# **Hero Animation**

The Hero should introduce the overall motion language of the product.

Animations should feel:

- smooth
- deliberate
- premium
- responsive

Avoid exaggerated entrances.

Avoid unnecessary delays.

Visitors should never wait for the interface.

---

# **Adaptive Transition**

Switching audiences should not replace the Hero.

Instead:

Current communication

↓

Adaptive decoding animation

↓

Updated communication

The transition should preserve continuity.

Nothing should jump.

Nothing should reload.

The visitor should feel that the Hero has adapted rather than changed.

---

# **Scroll Behaviour**

The Hero should naturally encourage scrolling.

Avoid explicit instructions such as:

“Scroll Down”

Instead, use motion, composition, and visual cues to encourage exploration.

Curiosity should drive interaction.

---

# **Trust Signals**

The Hero may include subtle trust indicators.

Examples:

- years of learning
- completed projects
- technologies
- availability
- response time

Trust signals should remain factual.

Avoid inflated numbers.

---

# **Content Guidelines**

Headlines should be:

- concise
- memorable
- authentic

Supporting paragraphs should:

- clarify
- reassure
- encourage

Avoid buzzwords.

Avoid unnecessary adjectives.

---

# **CMS Requirements**

Every editable element should be manageable through the Content Management System.

Including:

- headline
- subtitle
- paragraph
- CTA labels
- CTA links
- trust indicators
- availability
- profile imagery

Support identity-specific variants.

---

# **Analytics**

Potential anonymous events:

- Hero viewed
- Primary CTA clicked
- Secondary CTA clicked
- Audience selected
- Theme selected
- Adaptive transition completed

Analytics should measure interaction quality rather than simply page views.

---

# **Accessibility**

The Hero must support:

- keyboard navigation
- screen readers
- reduced motion
- sufficient contrast
- semantic heading structure

Animations should respect motion preferences.

---

# **Performance**

The Hero appears first.

It must feel instantaneous.

Avoid:

- blocking requests
- layout shifts
- oversized assets
- expensive animations

Performance here influences the perceived quality of the entire product.

---

# **Future Expansion**

Possible future additions include:

- contextual greetings
- returning visitor recognition
- adaptive recommendations
- live availability
- multilingual support
- seasonal messaging

Future additions should strengthen rather than complicate the Hero.

---

# **Definition of Success**

The Hero succeeds when visitors immediately understand:

Who I am.

Why this website feels different.

Why they should continue.

The Hero should create confidence before curiosity.

Curiosity before interaction.

Interaction before conversion.

---

# **Acceptance Criteria**

- The Hero establishes trust immediately.
- Audience selection precedes the Hero.
- Communication adapts while layout remains consistent.
- Azure and Noir influence tone without changing structure.
- Calls to action are audience-aware.
- Hero content is fully CMS-managed.
- Animations feel premium and purposeful.
- Accessibility requirements are satisfied.
- Performance remains excellent.
- Visitors naturally continue exploring the portfolio.
